Human-Computer Interaction is about
• Understand people (psychological, social aspects, human error.
We know how users behave under stress, so design for it!
• Understand computers ( limitations, capacities, tools, platforms)
• and their interaction …

HCI requires skills in motivating and persuading people
• Persuasion is ‘a non-coercive attempt to change attitudes or behaviors of people’ (Fogg, Cuellar and Danielson, 2008) Domains where persuasion matter are E-commerce, Education, Safety, Environment protection, Occupational productivity, Preventive health care, Fitness, Disease management, Activism, Personal relationships.
Computers can be used as
• Persuasive tools (by making it easier to do, increasing person’s abilities)
• Persuasive media (simulation)
• Persuasive social actors (social support, establish social norms, invoke social rules and dynamics)
